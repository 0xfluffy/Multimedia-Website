Spice Pantry

Indian Cook Book

Saffron

Saffron is the most expensive spice in the world. It is derived from the dried stigmas of the saffron crocus and has a deep auburn colour and sweet flavour.

Star Anise

Shaped like a flower this spice has seeds that impart an aniseed flavour and very strong taste.Star anise is a good source of antioxidants.

Curry Leaves

Aromatic and at their best when used fresh curry leaves are a relevant part of coastal cooking. Use whole to flavour oils and curries or lightly bruised.

Turmeric Powder

Powder derived from the turmeric plant; it has a bright yellow colour. ‘Haldi’ as it is known in India is used to lend colour to the dish as well as for its antiseptic properties

Kashmiri Red Chilli

Kashmiri chilli powder is a mild chilli powder used mainly for its vibrant red colour rather than heat. Perfect for tandoori chicken to give that natural colour.

Coriander Seeds

Roasting coriander seeds in a dry pan on low heat brings out the best flavour. The seeds gives a warm depth to dishes with lemony citrus notes.

Mustard Seeds

Black mustard seeds are commonly used in tempering & releasing a nutty flavour in the oil. Used in a lot of Gujarati, Marathi, Bengali & South Indian dishes.

Cloves

Derived from a dried flower bud of a tree provide a warm, sweet and peppery flavour. Can be used whole or powdered they have a long shelf life up to a year.

Cardamom

Green cardamom has a very distinct heady aroma used in sweet as well as savoury dishes. Available in stores in powdered as well as whole pods.
